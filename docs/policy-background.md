# Policy Background

The Outer Space Treaty remains the core framework for international space governance. This survey is designed to help a research team evaluate where treaty language may require clarification, amendment, supplementary protocol language, soft-law development, or alignment with selected Artemis Accords principles.

The survey does not provide legal advice and does not cite formal legal authority beyond the user's supplied framing. Internal author names identify research contributions and should not be treated as external legal authority.

Primary themes include benefit-sharing, non-appropriation, resource extraction, safety zones, peaceful purposes, dual-use systems, rescue obligations, state responsibility, liability, registration, environmental protection, transparency, inspection, joint mission responsibility, treaty modernization, and the Artemis Accords.
